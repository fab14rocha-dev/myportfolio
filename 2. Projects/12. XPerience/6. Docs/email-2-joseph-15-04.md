# Email 2 — Joseph LoBello
**Date:** 15 April 2026

## Summary

Joseph shared a detailed vision for how the onboarding/intake experience should feel. The big shift: he doesn't want it to feel like a form at all. He wants it to feel like an event — theatre, curtains, spotlights, a marquee sign.

---

## His Core Vision

**The flow he described (Birthday example):**

1. **Landing/Welcome screen**
   - "Welcome to our Award Winning Experiences"
   - A blue curtain opens with spotlights
   - Guest selects their occasion: Birthday / Family / Wedding / Group / Date Night / Other
   - No form — purely visual, interactive selection

2. **Birthday selected → Name + Date collection**
   - Ask: Name and date of birthday (e.g. Joseph, June 17th)
   - Still no form feel — conversational, immersive

3. **The big reveal screen**
   - Blue curtain opens on a theatre-like stage with a marquee sign
   - Reads: "Experience Greenville Welcomes Joseph — June 17th"
   - Countdown timer: 62 Days, 14 Hours, 3 Minutes
   - This is personalised in real time based on what they entered

4. **The ticket**
   - A "Million Dollar Ticket" appears — designed like a Super Bowl / Championship ticket
   - Personalised: Joseph / June 17th / Row 1 VIP / On Court Experience Package
   - High-drama, high-prestige feel

5. **Deeper intake questions**
   - Ask Joseph (or the person filling it out on his behalf) to share more about him:
     - Favorite Song
     - Favorite Food
     - Favorite Drink
     - Favorite Sport
     - Favorite Team
     - Favorite Athlete
     - Dream Job
   - These answers get stored and used to personalise the experience

---

## What This Tells Us

- He wants the **intake to be the experience** — not just a step before it
- The design is theatrical: curtains, stages, spotlights, marquee signs, countdown clocks
- Personalisation is central — the user's name and date should appear immediately on screen
- The "ticket" concept is a strong visual hook — premium, limited, VIP
- He mentioned "Award Winning" branding — that's part of the tone

---

## Open Questions (to discuss)

- Is the curtain/stage concept for the public-facing landing page, or specifically the intake flow after someone has already decided to book?
- Does the countdown timer serve a real purpose (e.g. urgency, countdown to their event) or is it purely theatrical?
- Who fills in the deeper questions — the guest themselves or the person booking for them?
- Does the ticket need to be downloadable / shareable?
- "Experience Greenville" — is this the actual brand name to use on the site?

---

## Notes

Joseph said he doesn't expect this to be built exactly as described — he's sharing the vision so we can explore what's possible. Treat this as a creative brief, not a spec.
