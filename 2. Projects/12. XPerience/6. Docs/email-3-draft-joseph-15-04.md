Hi Joseph,

Love the energy in your email. That vision is exciting and I want to share my honest take on it.

The curtain, the marquee, the countdown, the personalised ticket. Those are great ideas. I do think they belong at a different moment in the journey. Picture this: someone just bought their partner a 10th anniversary experience. Instead of a boring confirmation email, they land on a page with their partner's name on a marquee sign, a countdown to the date, and a VIP ticket with their name on it. That's when those elements hit the hardest, after the decision is made, as part of delivering the experience. It turns the confirmation into a moment.

I was thinking that we could start with a focused landing page, something simple and clean that is built to test the idea before we go bigger or expand to other ocasions. The page would have one job: speak directly to the right person and get them to take action.

Here's my thinking. The most valuable thing we can do is find out if the right person, when they land on the page, actually buys the experience. The way to learn that fast is to pick one specific audience and talk directly to them, not to everyone.

My pick is the anniversary audience. Specifically someone planning a surprise for a milestone anniversary, 5th, 10th, 15th. These people already know they want to do something special. They're willing to spend. And they're looking for something that feels worthy of the occasion.

If the page speaks directly to that person, "You want this anniversary to feel different. Here's how", the copy, the tone, and the offer all become much sharper. It stops feeling like a general service and starts feeling like it was made for them.

The goal is to get something live, send some traffic to it, and see if people take action. If they do, we have proof of concept and something real to build on. If they don't, we learn something fast without having built a full theatrical experience first.

What do you think about this approach?
